Our Story

About Indigo

Two women, two passions, one space where movement meets canvas.

A Space Born from
Shared Vision

Indigo Flow and Art was born when Katie Hughes Ryan and Julie Hughes discovered they shared more than a last name — they shared a vision. Katie, a classically trained Pilates instructor, and Julie, a fine artist drawn to motion and energy, found the perfect home for their dual practice at 1272 Pendleton Street in the Village of West Greenville.

What was once a single space became two worlds connected by old French doors: a vibrant gallery of original art on one side, and a light-filled studio with a fully equipped Gratz Pilates setup on the other. Students check in through the gallery, surrounded by Julie's work, before stepping into their practice. It's not by accident — at Indigo, art and movement aren't separate pursuits. They're two expressions of the same creative energy.

The Team

Meet the Co-Owners

Co-Owner · Indigo Pilates

Katie Hughes Ryan

Fully Certified Classical Pilates Instructor, trained at Power Pilates in Charlotte and New York City. Katie holds a 500-hour Registered Yoga Teacher certification and a 300-hour AYAMA (Applied Yoga Anatomy & Muscle Activation) certification.

Her background includes ballet, contemporary dance, competitive figure skating, yoga, and Pilates. She emphasizes a form-focused, athletic yet attainable approach — meeting every student exactly where they are and building authentic connection through movement.

Co-Owner · Indigo Art

Julie Hughes

Through her work, Julie strives to combine the everyday with the ephemeral — capturing real moments that are viewed from a surreal lens. Her themes are not concerned with what the world should be, but rather with what the world is, while choosing to see reality with slight distortions.

Her recent works focus on motion — specifically the art of bicycle racing. Speed, gravity-defying angles, and the raw energy of human movement put on canvas. It's a natural extension of the studio's philosophy: the body in motion is always art.
