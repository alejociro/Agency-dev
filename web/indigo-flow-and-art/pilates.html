The Method

What Is Classical Pilates?

Classical Pilates — originally called Contrology — is the complete system of exercises designed by Joseph Pilates in the early 20th century. Unlike modern adaptations, the classical method follows a specific order and philosophy: the whole body working in concert to build strength, flexibility, and control.

At Indigo, every student begins with private sessions to understand the fundamentals of connection before progressing through the full system. Our studio features a complete Gratz apparatus — the gold standard in Classical Pilates equipment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior Pilates experience?

Not at all. Every student at Indigo begins with private sessions to learn the fundamentals of connection. Whether you've never tried Pilates or you've practiced for years, Katie will meet you where you are and build from there.

What's the difference between Classical and Modern Pilates?

Classical Pilates follows the original system and exercise order designed by Joseph Pilates (called Contrology). Modern Pilates often modifies or reorganizes these exercises. At Indigo, we teach the authentic classical method on traditional Gratz apparatus.

What should I wear to my session?

Wear form-fitting, comfortable clothing that allows your instructor to see your alignment. Grip socks are recommended but not required. Avoid loose clothing that could catch on the apparatus.

What equipment does the studio have?

Indigo is a fully equipped Gratz Pilates studio — the gold standard for Classical Pilates apparatus. This includes the Reformer, Cadillac, Wunda Chair, High Chair, Ladder Barrel, Spine Corrector, and Mat.
