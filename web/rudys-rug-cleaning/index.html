Our Process

Five Steps to a Beautifully
Clean Rug

Every rug that comes through our doors receives the same careful, thorough treatment we've been perfecting since the 1960s.

01

Deep Dusting

We start by removing deeply embedded grit, sand, and dry soil that vacuuming alone can't reach. This protects the fibers during the wash.

02

Cold Water Soak

Your rug is soaked in cold water with our plant-based, soap-based detergent. Cold water is gentle on dyes and natural fibers.

03

Gentle Agitation

We carefully agitate the rug to release soil, odors, and stains without damaging the weave or pattern integrity.

04

Centrifuge Spinning

Excess moisture is removed through centrifuge spinning — faster and gentler than wringing, preserving the rug's shape and structure.

05

Climate-Controlled Drying

Your rug is hung to dry in our climate-controlled drying room, ensuring even drying with no mildew, shrinkage, or color bleeding.

Tips & Advice

Caring for Your Rugs
Between Cleanings

Simple habits can keep your rugs looking their best for years. Here are our top recommendations from over 60 years of rug care experience.

Rotate Your Rugs Regularly

Rotate your area rugs every 6 months to even out wear and sun exposure. This helps prevent uneven fading and ensures high-traffic areas don't break down faster than the rest.

Act Fast on Spills

Blot spills immediately with a clean white cloth — never rub. Work from the outside in to prevent spreading. Cold water works best for most stains. For stubborn marks, call us for advice.

Vacuum the Right Way

Vacuum both sides of your rug regularly, but avoid the fringe. Use a low-suction setting for delicate rugs like silk or antique weaves. This prevents grit from grinding into the fibers over time.

Use a Quality Rug Pad

A good rug pad prevents slipping, reduces wear, and protects your floors underneath. We custom-cut pads to fit your rug perfectly — ask us for a quote.
